To insert a direct threat, select a target and then click the <Insert Direct Threat> button located on the Control Bar. The software will add a new direct threat linked to the target and open a dialog box.

Type in the Name of your direct threat, and if desired, an ID (see hint below). You can also add additional information about the threat in the Details and/or Comments fields, and add evidence of the presence of the threat in the Evidence Notes field. If you plan to share your project with other people, you can also pick the entry that best describes your threat from the Standard Classification field that lists a taxonomy of all direct threats in the drop-down menu.

Once you close the dialog box, using the mouse or arrow keys, move the threat to wherever you want it to be on the diagram.

Hint: The Direct Threat ID field can be used for a brief user-defined code used to identify and sort your direct threats. One common system is to assign the letters DT and a number to each Direct Threat (e.g., DT1, DT2, etc.)

Hint: In most Miradi dialog boxes, the Details field is used to record a longer description of the factor, the Comments field is meant for discussion about the factor, and the Evidence Notes field is meant for evidence of the presence of the factor.

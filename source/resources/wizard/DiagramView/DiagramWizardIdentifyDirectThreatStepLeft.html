To insert a direct threat, select a target and then click the <Insert Direct Threat> button located on the Control Bar. The software will add a new direct threat linked to the target and open a dialogue box.

Type in the Name of your direct threat, and if desired, an ID (see hint below). You can also add additional information about the threat in the Details and/or Comments fields. If you plan to share your project with other people, you can also pick the entry that best describes your threat from the Standard Classification field that lists a taxonomy of all direct threats in the drop-down menu.

Once you close the dialogue box, using the mouse or arrow keys, move the threat to wherever you want it to be on the diagram.

Hint: Pink Direct Threat factors must be directly linked to a target. If you insert a direct threat without having a target selected, it will appear as an orange contributing factor until you link it to a target.

Hint: The Direct Threat ID field can be used for a short code used to identify and sort your direct threats. One common system is to assign the letters DT and a number to each Direct Threat (e.g., DT1, DT2, etc.)

Hint: In most Miradi dialogue boxes, the Details field is used to record a longer description of the factor, whereas the Comments field is meant for discussion about the factor.

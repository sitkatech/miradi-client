Explanation of Threat Rating N/A Option

The "N/A" checkbox allows users to designate a threat-target rating relationship as not applicable.

Miradi's general rule is to create a rating cell for any threat that is connected, directly or indirectly, to a conservation target. In some cases, this can lead to double counting of a threat effect.

Because all threats and targets are connected by arrows, Miradi generates a threat rating view that suggests all threat-target pairs should be rated:


However, rating all pairs may not be appropriate. If the only way that new roads affect jaguars is via forest habitat destruction, then the team would be rating that threat effect twice â€“ once for forests, and once again for jaguars. A similar situation would occur with new roads as they affect rivers. Here, a team might even wonder why that cell is considered a threat-target pair in their table because, in their context, roads do not directly affect rivers.

For such situations, the "N/A" checkbox allows a user to signal that this threat-target pair is not applicable for rating. It does not disable or delete rating entries, but serves instead as an override. If "N/A" is checked, any ratings entered for that threat-target pair are ignored but not lost (unchecking "N/A" would cause any prior ratings to reappear and be included in the summary roll-up).
